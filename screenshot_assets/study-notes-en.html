Biology I · Unit 3

Cell Structure and Function

Study notes · 12 key terms · Exam range p.84–101

1. Basic Concepts

Every living thing is made of cells, the smallest unit in which life processes occur. Based on whether they have a nucleus, cells are divided into prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells.

Prokaryotic cell

Has no nucleus enclosed by a membrane; its genetic material floats in the cytoplasm. Bacteria and archaea belong here.

Eukaryotic cell

Has a distinct, membrane-bound nucleus and various membrane organelles. Animals, plants, and fungi belong here.

2. Major Organelles

Mitochondria

The cell's "power plant," producing ATP through cellular respiration. It has its own DNA and a double-membrane structure.

Ribosome

The site of protein synthesis. It has no membrane and is either attached to the endoplasmic reticulum or free in the cytoplasm.

ER & Golgi apparatus

Responsible for synthesizing, processing, and transporting materials. Substances made in the ER are refined and secreted by the Golgi apparatus.

3. Key Summary

  • The cell membrane is a phospholipid bilayer that selectively controls what enters and leaves.
  • Unlike animal cells, plant cells have a cell wall and chloroplasts.
  • The nucleus stores genetic information and directs the cell's activity.
